About

The Jewish Museum was designened by polish architect Daniel Libeskind. The shape of the building was a matrix that represents Jewish and German lineage based on a distorted Star of David. The starbeing a notable Jewish symbol.

The Jewish Museum is a composite of 5 levels, the underground/entrance floor, ground floor, first, second and third floor. the building is also organized by voids. Voids are negative spaces, that represent the sense of loss and horror the Jews have experienced in Germany. The Voided spaces extend through the entire museum from the underground level to the roof, these concrete spaces lack heating, cooling and sufficient lighting creating a spatial and sensory effect.

The Architect

Daniel Libeskind (1946–)

Photo of Daniel Libeskind by 準建築人手札網站 Forgemind ArchiMedia in Flickr.

Daniel Libeskind is a Polish born architect. His parents migrated to Poland during the Nazi regime in Germany. The Jewish Museum was Libeskind first building comission. Since Libeskind has been involved in designing notable structures such as the MOMA museum and World Trade Center.

Key Features

Garden of Exile

In the garden, 49 slabs of stone, or stelae, are arranged in a seven by seven grid that stands on a large concrete disc that is tilted to an angle. On top of these slabes, Russian willow oak was planted to symbolize hope.

Holocaust Tower

Pentagonal free-standing concrete silo. The composition of the tower creates a sense of isolation and dread. No central air conditioning reaches the tower. The only light that shines in the tower is the sliver of light from the top.

Memory Void

The memory void is filled with steel plates cut into faces. This is part of the Fallen Leaves art installment. It represent the painful loss of countless Jewish who were vitims of Holocaust.

FAQS

How long did it take to complete the Jewish Museum?
12 Years.
Libeskind was commissioned to construct the museum by winning an anonymous design competition in 1989. The Jewish Museum Berlin finished construction in 1998 and opened in 2001 to the public.
Where is the museum located?
The museum is located on the site of Lindenstrasse, a main road in the Friedrichstadt district of Berlin. The area houses historical judicial and administrative buildings. The Berlin wall is a 5 minute drive away.
What are some notable design features of the museum?
  • Zigzag building plan
  • Slash metallic facade
  • Angular interior
  • Mesh of old and new structures

If you are interest in learning more or visiting the Jewish Museum of Berlin visit the Official Site.
